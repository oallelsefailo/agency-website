Adobe Commerce Analytics: What Native Reporting Misses

Adobe Commerce has useful built-in reporting, especially for orders and customers. The hard part is seeing that store data next to traffic, search visibility, and the issues your team should review this week.

What Adobe Commerce reporting does well

Adobe Commerce, formerly Magento Commerce, includes a stronger reporting layer than the open-source Magento edition. Its Business Intelligence tools cover sales reports, customer segmentation, product performance, and some marketing attribution. For a merchant with a dedicated analytics person, those tools give a useful internal read on what sold, who bought it, and where margin landed.

The order data is a real strength. Revenue, tax, refunds, and product-level sales come from the commerce database, so the numbers are close to the source. The BI layer also helps with visualization and scheduled reports, which is a step up from the CSV exports many Magento teams still live with.

Where the gap opens up

Adobe Commerce reporting has the same built-in limit as most store reporting tools: it sees what happened inside the store. It does not show how shoppers got there, which pages are gaining or losing search demand, or what changed before the order happened. That creates a few familiar problems.

Traffic context is missing. The BI dashboard can tell you a product line dropped 18% month over month. It cannot tell you whether organic traffic to that product page also fell, whether paid search got more expensive, or whether direct traffic rose while organic slipped. Those are different problems with different fixes. Without GA4 connected to order data, the cause is usually a guess.

Search visibility is disconnected. Adobe Commerce connects with Google Shopping and some ad channels, but Google Search Console is not part of the native reporting surface. That means someone still has to check Search Console to see whether a page lost impressions, dropped in average position, or kept impressions but lost CTR. A technical SEO issue, a weaker search snippet, and seasonal demand all look different when the data is side by side.

The weekly readout is still manual. Adobe Commerce BI can show reports. It does not read across GA4, Search Console, and order data and summarize what changed. The operator still opens each source, compares numbers, writes notes, and builds the review by hand.

How to fill the gap without building a custom stack

Most teams either build a custom reporting pipeline or connect a Magento-native dashboard. The build path usually means sending GA4 into Looker Studio or a warehouse, pulling Search Console through an API or connector, and layering it over Adobe Commerce order exports. It can work, but it needs maintenance. APIs change, fields drift, schemas move, and the person who built the dashboard becomes the person everyone depends on.
